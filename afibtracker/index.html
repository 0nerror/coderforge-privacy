AfibTracker — Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 22, 2026

AfibTracker (“the app”) is developed by Codersforge. This policy explains what data the app handles and how. By using the app you agree to this policy.

Health and heart-rate data

AfibTracker estimates your heart rate and rhythm using your device’s camera (photoplethysmography). Camera image frames are processed in memory on your device in real time and are never saved and never transmitted off your device. Only the derived results — heart rate values, a small waveform summary, an “irregular rhythm” indicator, and any triggers you log — are stored locally on your device in the app’s database. We do not have access to this data, and the app does not back it up to the cloud.

Sharing reports

When you choose to export a PDF report, the file is generated on your device and shared only through the apps you select (for example, email). We do not receive it.

Advertising

The app displays ads through Google AdMob. To serve ads, the Google Mobile Ads SDK may collect and transmit certain data (such as device information, IP address, and an advertising identifier) as described in Google’s Privacy Policy: https://policies.google.com/privacy. We request non-personalized ads by default, and where required we show a consent form (Google User Messaging Platform) so you can manage your choices.

Data we do NOT collect

We do not require an account, we do not collect your name or email, and we use no third-party analytics beyond what the advertising SDK above requires.

Not a medical device

AfibTracker is for information and tracking purposes only. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any condition. If you think something is wrong with your health, seek medical treatment.

Children

The app is not directed to children under 13.

Changes

We may update this policy; the effective date above will change accordingly.
